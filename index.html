Framework: SIM-ONE Philosophy

SIM-ONE is a pragmatic philosophy for governed cognition: Systems, Interfaces, Metrics — Oriented to Norms and Evidence. It emphasizes building cognitive tools that are auditable by design, aligned to explicit norms, and measured against real outcomes.

Systems

Design for modularity, traceability, versioning, and graceful degradation.

Interfaces

Shape behavior with prompts, controls, and affordances that prevent failure modes.

Metrics

Measure lagging outcomes and leading indicators; monitor shifts and drift.

Oriented to Norms

Ground cognition in policy, law, and domain standards that can be cited.

Evidence

Use tests, counterfactuals, audits, and causal analyses to justify changes.

Five Laws of Governed Cognition

  1. Attribution Law: Every answer should show its sources and uncertainty.
  2. Control Law: Critical paths must have guardrails, overrides, and audit logs.
  3. Context Law: Systems must bind to the right context window and data policy.
  4. Update Law: Models, prompts, and policies evolve together under change control.
  5. Impact Law: Optimize for downstream human decisions, not just model scores.
